WGS84 Projection

The circles indicate some latitudinal distortion as the latitude moves further away from the equator. The longitude is not distorted.

Aitoff Projection

The projection is globular, and you can tell because the latitudinal and longitudinal lines are curved. The circles become more distorted as you move away from the prime meridian and the equator.

WGS84 Projection

This projection has equal distortion between the latitude and longitude because the circles grow proportionally as you move away from the equator and prime meridian.

Sphere Winkel I Projection

This projection is globular because the lines of longitude curve as you move away from the equator. Overall, the projection has some distortion as you move away from the equator and some slight distortion in the highest longitudes.

World Cylindrical Equal Area Projection

In this projection there is significant distortion in the latitudes furthest away from the equator. Land close to the poles looks very wide.

World Equidistant Conic Projection

Compared to the other projections, this one is unique. The view is similar to looking at a globe from the top, as the north pole is in the center of the projection. As you move away from the north pole, there is more distortion.

North Pole Azimuthal Equidistant Projection

Similar to the previous projection, this one is centered on the north pole. They differ slighly in the severity of the longitudinal distortion, as this projection is slightly more equal around the globe than the last.

World Aitoff Projection

In this projection, the farther away from the equator and prime meridian you go the more distorted the projection is.

Sphere Times Projection

The circles in this projection increase in size proportionally, revealing that the longitude and latitude are distorted similarly as you move further away from the equator and prime meridian.
